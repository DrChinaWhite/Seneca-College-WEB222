Quick Facts

Common Name: Red Fox

Scientific Name: Vulpes vulpes

Type: Mammal

Diet: Omnivore

Average Life Span In The Wild: 2 to 4 years

Size: 90-105 cm

Weight: 2.2-14 kg

Description

Not all red foxes are in-fact red, there are many colour variations including brown, black and silver. With the latter being colloquially known as "silver foxes."

Red foxes are small, canine creatures that have pointy features and a slender body. Northern foxes fur is dense and fluffy and features silky guard hairs. Whereas their southern counterparts fur is sparser and coarser. Their fur is typically reddish-brown; however, their ears and legs are black. A distinguishing feature of the red fox is its white-tipped tail.

What Does The Fox Say?

Contrary to popular belief led by the artist Ylvis in 2013 foxes do not communicate through these phrases:
  • Ring-ding-ding-ding-dingeringeding!
  • Wa-pa-pa-pa-pa-pa-pow!
  • Hatee-hatee-hatee-ho!
  • Joff-tchoff-tchoffo-tchoffo-tchoff!

Rather foxes tend to communicate through body language, vocalizations, and scent.

Body Language

Foxes body language can be separated into two categories dominance and submissive. The body language consists of movements of the ears, tail, and postures. The line between aggressive and playful is not a straightforward task to determine by humans. One key indication is to look at the tail, if it is forming an 'upside-down U-shape' it could indicate play.

Vocalizations

Red Foxes have a large vocal range spanning five octaves. Using their vocal cords they are able to make a variety of different sounds including barks, screams, howls, squeals, and 'gekkering'. These vocalizations can be further categorized into contact calls and interaction calls. Contact calls are three to five syllable barking of "wow wow wow" often when two foxes are approaching one another. Interaction calls are usually high pitched whines which signifies submissiveness.

Scent

Red foxes have a strong sense of smell allowing them to communicate through scent. They use chemicals from their urine and scents from their glands to mark their territories, and food storages. When two foxes meet, they will often smell each other's glands and urinate on each other to mark themselves as friends, or partners.

Diet

Red foxes are omnivorous creatures and have a highly varied diet including:

  • voles
  • mice
  • raspberries
  • insects

Primary prey include small rodents, secondary prey include birds and insects, and occasionally they consume fruits. Red foxes prefer to hunt in the early morning hours or late evening. As red foxes are extremely possessive of their food they will typically forage alone; however, in areas with plenty of resources they may group together to share the bounty. They are very versatile and adaptable to their surroundings, as such are capable of hunting and foraging during the winter months.
